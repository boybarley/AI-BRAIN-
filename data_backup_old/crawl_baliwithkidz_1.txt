e-VOA apply online before arrival
Visa On Arrival or Single Entry Visitor Visa for Tourism Purpose for foreigners who will enter Indonesia.
e-VOA is a single-entry visa which is valid for a 30 day stay in Indonesia with the purpose of Tourism, Government Visit, Business Meeting, Goods Purchasing, or Transit only.
Holders of travel document other than passport (Laissez Passer, Titre de voyage, Travel Document, Document of Identity, Certificate of Identity, Alien Passport, Alien Travel Document, Emergency Travel Document, Emergency Passport; or Temporary Passport) are not eligible to apply e-VOA.
The e-VOA fee is IDR 500.000,00 plus a service fee.  Please note that every payment via Debit/Credit Card will also incur additional fees according to the financial institution’s regulations.

New Health Pass Requirement
New Health Pass Requirement for International Travelers to Indonesia
Important update from the Indonesian Government, effective immediately, following the classification of Mpox as a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) by the WHO on August 14, 2024.
All passengers and aircraft personnel traveling from abroad are now required to fill in a Health Pass prior to entry into Indonesia.  Please complete the form before boarding your flight to Bali.
Are children required to fill out the SATUSEHAT Health Pass?
Every traveler is required to fill in the SATUSEHAT Health Pass including children. For children, parents or guardians can help fill it in and can save the QR Code of the child's SATUSEHAT Health Pass.
Bali tourist tax
The Bali tourist tax, officially called the "Tourist Levy", is a mandatory fee of IDR 150,000 (approximately USD 10) that all international tourists must pay when visiting Bali.
Amount: IDR 150,000 (~USD 10)
Who needs to pay: All international tourists, regardless of visa type or nationality, including children
When to pay: Ideally before arrival via the official site Or on arrival at designated counters in Bali’s Ngurah Rai International Airport or seaports
What’s it for: The levy is aimed at: Supporting cultural preservation, Funding sustainable tourism initiatives, Helping to protect Bali’s environment and heritage
Important Notes: It’s a one-time payment per visit Domestic tourists (Indonesians or KITAS/KITAP holders) are exempt You won’t be denied entry if you haven’t paid in advance, but you will be asked to pay on arrival
